Most travel sites ask the same opening question: *where do you want to go?* It feels natural — but it quietly does something unhelpful. The moment you name a place, you've thrown away every other trip that might have been a better fit for your money and your mood.

## The problem with destination-first

When you start with a destination, price becomes an afterthought you discover at the end — often a disappointing one. You've already fallen for the idea of a place before you know whether it fits your budget, your dates, or the season.

Budget-first flips the order. You start with the two things you almost always know — what you can spend and the kind of experience you want — and let the destination be the answer instead of the question.

## Why an engine does this better than a search box

A person can only hold a few destinations in their head at once. An engine can weigh hundreds against your budget at the same time, factoring in:

- flight prices from your home airport
- where your money stretches furthest this season
- weather and events for your dates

The result is a short list of places that actually fit — including ones you'd never have typed into a search box.
