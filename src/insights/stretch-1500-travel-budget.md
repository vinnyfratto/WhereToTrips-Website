A $1,500 budget can mean a cramped long weekend or a genuinely great week abroad. The difference is rarely luck — it's being flexible about the things that move price the most.

## 1. Be flexible on where, not just when

Shifting your dates saves a little. Shifting your *destination* saves a lot. The same week that's expensive in one country is a bargain a short flight away. Start from the budget and let the place flex.

## 2. Travel in the shoulder season

The weeks just before and after peak season often have the same weather at a fraction of the price — and far thinner crowds. Autumn and late spring are the quiet superpowers of budget travel.

## 3. Fly from your real cheapest airport

Your home airport isn't always your cheapest one. A nearby hub can swing a fare by hundreds of dollars. Always compare.

## 4. Spend on the experience, save on the bed

You won't remember the hotel lobby. Put the money where the memories are — the meal, the dive, the guide — and keep the room simple.
