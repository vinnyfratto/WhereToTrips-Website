Every group trip starts with excitement and ends in a 200-message thread where nothing gets booked. The problem usually isn't the people — it's that the planning has no structure.

## Why group trips stall

Three things quietly kill group plans: everyone has a different budget, everyone has different dates, and everyone's departing from a different city. A group chat surfaces all of that noise at once, but it has no way to resolve it. So the trip drifts.

## A better order of operations

The groups that actually go tend to follow the same sequence:

1. **Collect constraints privately first.** Each person's real budget and dates — before anyone anchors on a place.
2. **Find the overlap.** Look for destinations that work for the whole group, not just the loudest planner.
3. **Vote, don't debate.** Put a short list to a vote and let it decide.
4. **Book together.** One flow, one deadline, no "I'll book mine later."
